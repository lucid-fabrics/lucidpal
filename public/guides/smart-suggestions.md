# Smart Suggestions

LucidPal learns what you use it for and surfaces relevant prompts each time you start a new chat.

## What you see

When you open a new conversation, up to four suggestion chips appear in the centre of the screen. Tap any chip to send it instantly as your first message.

The chips are not random. They are ranked based on what you have actually been asking LucidPal about — the more you use the app, the more accurately they reflect your habits.

## How it learns

Every time you send a message, LucidPal notes the topic. Over time it builds a picture of your usage: do you mostly ask about your calendar, draft emails, log habits, or think through decisions? The most relevant topics float to the top.

Recent messages count more than older ones. Something you asked about this morning weighs roughly ten times more than something you asked about last month.

Nothing is sent anywhere. The analysis runs entirely on your iPhone and is never shared.

## Thread continuation

If you started a conversation in the last 48 hours but only sent one or two messages — enough to start something but not finish it — LucidPal will offer to pick it up:

> _Still on: [your message]…_

Tap it to jump straight back into that thread in a new chat. This is especially useful when you get interrupted mid-thought.

## Calendar suggestions

One chip is always reserved for your calendar. It adapts to the time of day and what is actually in your schedule:

- **Morning with events:** "Walk me through today's agenda"
- **Evening with a busy tomorrow:** "Prep me for tomorrow"
- **Midday, next meeting approaching:** "Prep me for [meeting name]"
- **Free day:** "Do I have anything today?"

If you have not given LucidPal calendar access, this chip shows a general focus prompt instead. See the [Calendar guide](./calendar) to connect your calendar.

## First-time experience

When you are new to LucidPal (fewer than five messages sent), the chips show a curated set of discovery prompts — one for each major feature — so you can explore what the app can do. Once you have sent a few messages, the personalised suggestions take over.

## Tips

- **Use the chips, then rephrase.** Tapping a chip sends it as-is. You can always add more detail in your follow-up message.
- **The more specific your messages, the better the suggestions.** "Prep me for my 3pm standup with the team" trains the calendar domain more precisely than "what's next".
- **Chips update every session.** Each new chat recalculates suggestions based on your latest activity, so they stay fresh.
